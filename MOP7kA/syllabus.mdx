# Machine Learning Foundations

## Overview
In this first week, we will lay the foundation for your machine learning journey. We will start by introducing the fundamental concepts and principles of machine learning. You will learn about different types of machine learning algorithms and their applications. Additionally, we will dive into the math and theory behind machine learning, covering topics such as linear algebra, calculus, and probability theory.

## The Group Experience
During this week, you will participate in a hands-on workshop where you will implement basic machine learning algorithms and gain practical experience. You will also have a one-on-one challenge-solving session with a fellow group member to reinforce your understanding of the concepts.

## Outcomes
- Understanding of the core concepts and principles of machine learning
- Familiarity with the mathematical foundations of machine learning
- Ability to implement basic machine learning algorithms

# Biological Data Modeling

## Overview
In the second week, we will shift our focus to biological data modeling. You will learn about the different types of biological data, including genomics, proteomics, and metabolomics. We will explore the challenges and opportunities in modeling biological data and discuss various techniques for data preprocessing specific to biological datasets.

## The Group Experience
During this week, you will engage in a stimulating discussion with your group members to explore real-world examples of biological data modeling. You will also have the opportunity to work on a group project where you will apply data preprocessing techniques to a biological dataset.

## Outcomes
- Understanding of the unique characteristics of biological data
- Knowledge of data preprocessing techniques for biological datasets
- Ability to apply data preprocessing techniques to biological datasets

# Feature Selection and Statistical Modeling

## Overview
In the third week, we will delve into feature selection and extraction for biological data. You will learn how to identify relevant features and reduce dimensionality in biological datasets. We will also explore different statistical modeling techniques for biological data, including linear regression, logistic regression, and decision trees.

## The Group Experience
During this week, you will participate in an intense hackathon where you will work collaboratively with your group members to develop feature selection algorithms for biological datasets. You will also have a one-on-one challenge-solving session to reinforce your understanding of statistical modeling techniques.

## Outcomes
- Ability to perform feature selection and extraction for biological datasets
- Knowledge of statistical modeling techniques for biological data
- Proficiency in implementing feature selection algorithms

# Evaluating and Validating Biological Data Models

## Overview
In the fourth week, we will focus on evaluating and validating biological data models. You will learn about different evaluation metrics and techniques for assessing the performance of machine learning models on biological datasets. We will also discuss cross-validation and bootstrapping methods for model validation.

## The Group Experience
During this week, you will engage in a stimulating discussion with your group members to explore the challenges and best practices in evaluating biological data models. You will also have the opportunity to work on a group project where you will evaluate and validate machine learning models on a biological dataset.

## Outcomes
- Understanding of evaluation metrics for biological data models
- Knowledge of validation techniques for machine learning models
- Ability to evaluate and validate machine learning models on biological datasets

# Incorporating Domain Knowledge into Biological Data Models

## Overview
In the fifth week, we will explore the importance of incorporating domain knowledge into biological data models. You will learn how to leverage prior knowledge and expert insights to improve the performance and interpretability of machine learning models in the context of biotech research. We will discuss techniques such as feature engineering and domain-specific regularization.

## The Group Experience
During this week, you will participate in a hands-on workshop where you will work collaboratively with your group members to incorporate domain knowledge into biological data models. You will also have a one-on-one challenge-solving session to reinforce your understanding of the techniques discussed.

## Outcomes
- Ability to incorporate domain knowledge into biological data models
- Knowledge of feature engineering techniques for biological datasets
- Proficiency in applying domain-specific regularization techniques

# Introduction to Deep Learning

## Overview
In the sixth week, we will introduce you to the exciting field of deep learning. You will learn about the basic concepts and principles of neural networks and their architecture. We will discuss the different types of neural networks, including feedforward neural networks, recurrent neural networks, and convolutional neural networks.

## The Group Experience
During this week, you will engage in a stimulating discussion with your group members to explore the applications and potential of deep learning in biotech research. You will also have the opportunity to work on a group project where you will implement a basic neural network for a biological data task.

## Outcomes
- Understanding of the basic concepts and principles of deep learning
- Knowledge of different types of neural networks and their applications
- Ability to implement a basic neural network for biological data analysis

# Understanding Neural Networks and Optimization Algorithms

## Overview
In the seventh week, we will dive deeper into neural networks and optimization algorithms in deep learning. You will learn about activation functions and their role in neural network architectures. We will also discuss different optimization algorithms, such as gradient descent and its variants, for training neural networks.

## The Group Experience
During this week, you will participate in an intense hackathon where you will experiment with different activation functions and optimization algorithms for neural networks. You will also have a one-on-one challenge-solving session to reinforce your understanding of these concepts.

## Outcomes
- Understanding of activation functions and their impact on neural network performance
- Knowledge of optimization algorithms for training neural networks
- Proficiency in experimenting with different activation functions and optimization algorithms

# Convolutional Neural Networks for Image Classification

## Overview
In the eighth week, we will focus on convolutional neural networks (CNNs) for image classification. You will learn about the architecture and components of CNNs, such as convolutional layers, pooling layers, and fully connected layers. We will discuss techniques for training and fine-tuning CNNs on biological image datasets.

## The Group Experience
During this week, you will engage in a stimulating discussion with your group members to explore the applications of CNNs in biotech research. You will also have the opportunity to work on a group project where you will implement a CNN for image classification on a biological dataset.

## Outcomes
- Understanding of the architecture and components of convolutional neural networks
- Knowledge of techniques for training and fine-tuning CNNs on biological image datasets
- Ability to implement a CNN for image classification on biological datasets

# Recurrent Neural Networks for Sequence Data Analysis

## Overview
In the ninth week, we will shift our focus to recurrent neural networks (RNNs) for sequence data analysis. You will learn about the architecture and working principles of RNNs, including long short-term memory (LSTM) and gated recurrent unit (GRU) networks. We will discuss applications of RNNs in analyzing biological sequence data.

## The Group Experience
During this week, you will participate in a hands-on workshop where you will implement RNNs for sequence data analysis on biological datasets. You will also have a one-on-one challenge-solving session to reinforce your understanding of RNNs.

## Outcomes
- Understanding of the architecture and working principles of recurrent neural networks
- Knowledge of LSTM and GRU networks for sequence data analysis
- Ability to implement RNNs for analyzing biological sequence data

# Generative Adversarial Networks for Data Generation

## Overview
In the tenth week, we will explore generative adversarial networks (GANs) for data generation. You will learn about the architecture and training process of GANs. We will discuss applications of GANs in generating synthetic biological data and augmenting existing datasets.

## The Group Experience
During this week, you will engage in a stimulating discussion with your group members to explore the potential and ethical considerations of using GANs in biotech research. You will also have the opportunity to work on a group project where you will implement a GAN for data generation on a biological dataset.

## Outcomes
- Understanding of the architecture and training process of generative adversarial networks
- Knowledge of applications of GANs in generating synthetic biological data
- Ability to implement a GAN for data generation on biological datasets

# Transfer Learning and Fine-tuning in Deep Learning

## Overview
In the eleventh week, we will focus on transfer learning and fine-tuning in deep learning. You will learn how to leverage pre-trained models and transfer knowledge from one task to another. We will discuss techniques for fine-tuning deep learning models on biological datasets.

## The Group Experience
During this week, you will participate in an intense hackathon where you will explore transfer learning and fine-tuning techniques for deep learning models in biotech research. You will also have a one-on-one challenge-solving session to reinforce your understanding of these concepts.

## Outcomes
- Understanding of transfer learning and fine-tuning in deep learning
- Knowledge of techniques for leveraging pre-trained models in biotech research
- Proficiency in fine-tuning deep learning models on biological datasets

You will also have the opportunity to work on a group project where you will optimize the hyperparameters of a deep learning model on a biological dataset.

## Outcomes

- Knowledge of techniques for optimizing hyperparameters in deep learning models
- Ability to perform hyperparameter tuning for deep learning models on biological datasets
