# Machine Learning Fundamentals

We are excited to present you with the initial draft of your experience. This experience is designed to provide you with a comprehensive understanding of machine learning and its applications. Each week will build upon the previous one, allowing you to gradually advance your knowledge and skills in this field.

## The Group Experience

Once a week, you will have the opportunity to participate in an immersive experience with your group. These sessions will vary between hands-on workshops, intense hackathons, and stimulating discussions. Your weekends will never be the same! Additionally, you will be paired up with a fellow group member for a one-on-one challenge-solving session, rotating partners every week to keep things fresh.

## Outcomes

- Gain a solid understanding of machine learning concepts and algorithms
- Develop practical skills in implementing and training machine learning models
- Learn how to evaluate and select the best models for different tasks
- Acquire knowledge in natural language processing (NLP) techniques and applications
- Understand the process of reading and analyzing research papers in the field of machine learning

## The Roadmap

### Week 1: Introduction to Machine Learning

In this week, you will be introduced to the fundamentals of machine learning. You will learn about the basic concepts, types of machine learning algorithms, and the overall workflow of building machine learning models. The objective of this week is to provide you with a solid foundation for the rest of the experience.

### Week 2: Supervised Learning Algorithms

This week will focus on supervised learning algorithms. You will explore various algorithms such as linear regression, logistic regression, and support vector machines. You will learn how to train these models and evaluate their performance. By the end of the week, you will be able to apply supervised learning algorithms to solve real-world problems.

### Week 3: Unsupervised Learning Algorithms

In week 3, you will dive into unsupervised learning algorithms. You will explore clustering algorithms, such as K-means and hierarchical clustering, as well as dimensionality reduction techniques like principal component analysis (PCA). You will learn how to apply these algorithms to discover patterns and structures in unlabeled data.

### Week 4: Neural Networks and Deep Learning

This week will introduce you to neural networks and deep learning. You will learn about the basic architecture of neural networks, activation functions, and backpropagation. You will also explore deep learning frameworks like PyTorch and learn how to build and train deep neural networks for various tasks.

### Week 5: Model Evaluation and Selection

In week 5, you will focus on evaluating and selecting machine learning models. You will learn about different evaluation metrics, cross-validation techniques, and strategies for model selection. You will gain practical experience in assessing the performance of different models and selecting the best one for a given task.

### Week 6: Regularization and Optimization

This week will cover regularization and optimization techniques in machine learning. You will learn about regularization methods like L1 and L2 regularization, as well as optimization algorithms such as gradient descent and stochastic gradient descent. You will understand how these techniques can improve model performance and prevent overfitting.

### Week 7: Support Vector Machines and Decision Trees

In week 7, you will delve deeper into support vector machines (SVM) and decision trees. You will learn about the theory behind SVM and how to apply it to both classification and regression problems. You will also explore decision trees and random forests, understanding their advantages and limitations.

### Week 8: Clustering Algorithms and Dimensionality Reduction

This week will focus on clustering algorithms and dimensionality reduction techniques. You will learn about algorithms like DBSCAN and Gaussian mixture models for clustering, as well as dimensionality reduction techniques like t-SNE and autoencoders. You will gain practical experience in applying these techniques to real-world datasets.

### Week 9: PyTorch Core Concepts and Tensor Operations

In week 9, you will dive into PyTorch, a popular deep learning framework. You will learn about the core concepts of PyTorch, including tensors, computational graphs, and automatic differentiation. You will also explore tensor operations and how to manipulate tensors for building neural networks.

### Week 10: Neural Network Architecture and Training

This week will focus on neural network architecture and training. You will learn about different types of neural network architectures, including convolutional neural networks (CNNs) and recurrent neural networks (RNNs). You will also gain practical experience in training neural networks using PyTorch.

### Week 11: Data Loading and Preprocessing

In week 11, you will learn about data loading and preprocessing techniques. You will explore methods for handling different types of data, such as images, text, and time series. You will also learn how to preprocess data to prepare it for training machine learning models.

### Week 12: Model Evaluation and Validation

This week will cover advanced techniques for model evaluation and validation. You will learn about techniques like stratified sampling, k-fold cross-validation, and hyperparameter tuning. You will gain practical experience in evaluating and validating machine learning models using these techniques.

### Week 13: Transfer Learning and GPU Acceleration

In week 13, you will explore transfer learning techniques and GPU acceleration. You will learn how to leverage pre-trained models for transfer learning and fine-tuning. You will also understand how to utilize GPUs to accelerate the training and inference of deep learning models.

### Week 14: NLP Text Preprocessing and Tokenization

This week will focus on natural language processing (NLP) techniques. You will learn about text preprocessing techniques, including tokenization, stemming, and lemmatization. You will gain practical experience in preprocessing text data for NLP tasks.

### Week 15: Word Embeddings and Language Modeling

In week 15, you will dive into word embeddings and language modeling. You will learn about techniques like word2vec and GloVe for representing words as dense vectors. You will also explore language modeling techniques, such as recurrent neural networks (RNNs) and transformers.

### Week 16: Named Entity Recognition and Sentiment Analysis

This week will cover named entity recognition (NER) and sentiment analysis. You will learn how to extract named entities from text and classify sentiment in text data. You will gain practical experience in building NER and sentiment analysis models using machine learning and deep learning techniques.

### Week 17: Text Classification and Sequence Labeling

In week 17, you will focus on text classification and sequence labeling tasks. You will learn about different approaches for text classification, including bag-of-words, TF-IDF, and deep learning models. You will also explore sequence labeling tasks like part-of-speech tagging and named entity recognition.

### Week 18: Machine Translation and Question Answering

This week will cover machine translation and question answering. You will learn about machine translation techniques, including statistical machine translation and neural machine translation. You will also explore question answering models, such as reading comprehension models and transformer-based models.

### Week 19: Model Implementation and Architecture

In week 19, you will learn about model implementation and architecture design. You will understand the process of choosing a model architecture based on the task requirements. You will gain practical experience in implementing different machine learning and deep learning models.

### Week 20: Defining Loss Functions and Optimizing Model Parameters

This week will focus on defining loss functions and optimizing model parameters. You will learn about different loss functions for classification, regression, and other tasks. You will also explore optimization algorithms like gradient descent and Adam for updating model parameters.

### Week 21: Handling Overfitting and Hyperparameter Tuning

In week 21, you will learn how to handle overfitting and perform hyperparameter tuning. You will explore techniques like regularization, early stopping, and dropout to prevent overfitting. You will also learn how to tune hyperparameters to optimize model performance.

### Week 22: Model Interpretation and Visualization

This week will cover model interpretation and visualization techniques. You will learn how to interpret the predictions of machine learning models and explain their decisions. You will also explore visualization techniques for understanding model behavior and analyzing model performance.

### Week 23: Model Deployment and Serving

In week 23, you will learn about model deployment and serving. You will explore different approaches for deploying machine learning models, including cloud platforms and containerization. You will gain practical experience in serving models and making predictions in real-time.

### Week 24: Reading Research Papers and Experimental Methodology

In the final week, you will focus on reading research papers in the field of machine learning. You will learn how to understand the structure and format of research papers. You will also gain skills in identifying key contributions, evaluating experimental methodology, and interpreting results and conclusions.

### Week 25: Analyzing Limitations and Future Directions

In the last week of the experience, you will critically analyze the limitations of research papers and discuss future directions in the field of machine learning. You will learn how to identify potential improvements and extensions to existing work. You will also gain skills in summarizing and presenting the findings of research papers.

### Week 26: Reproducing Experiments and Final Project

In the final week, you will focus on reproducing experiments and working on your final project. You will have the opportunity to apply your knowledge and skills to reproduce experiments from research papers or work on a project of your choice. You will receive guidance and support from your group and instructors throughout this process.

We are looking forward to hearing your thoughts and feedback on this experience!
